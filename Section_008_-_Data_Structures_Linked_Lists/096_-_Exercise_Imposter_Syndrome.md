Here is practical advice about imposter syndrome, drawn from the instructor’s message and common best practices:

- Recognize it as a symptom of learning, not a lack of ability. Feeling like an imposter means you are stretching beyond your comfort zone, which is exactly where growth happens.
- Compare yourself only to your past self, not to industry experts. Everyone starts as a beginner.
- Use the feeling as encouragement. It proves you are doing something worthwhile and challenging.
- Take breaks when you feel overwhelmed. Step away, get a coffee or tea, then return refreshed.
- Teach what you have just learned. Helping others on Discord or in study groups reinforces your own knowledge and reduces self-doubt. Explaining a concept to someone else forces you to organize your thoughts and see what you actually understand.
- Remember that nobody is perfect. Even experienced professionals have moments of uncertainty.
- Practice regularly. With repetition, the uncomfortable feelings fade. Confidence comes from action, not from waiting until you feel ready.

If imposter syndrome ever stops you from applying for a job or speaking up, remind yourself: “Not yet” is not “never.” You are on the path, and that is enough.
