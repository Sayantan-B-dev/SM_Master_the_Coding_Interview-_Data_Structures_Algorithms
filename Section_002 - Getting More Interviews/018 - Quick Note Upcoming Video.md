This quickstart guide will walk you through creating a user site hosted at `username.github.io`.

### 🚀 Creating Your Site

1.  **Create a new repository**: In the upper-right corner of any page on GitHub, select the **+** drop-down menu and click **New repository**.
2.  **Name it correctly**: Enter `username.github.io` as the repository name, replacing `username` with your actual GitHub username.
3.  **Set it up**: Choose your repository's visibility (public or private). **Toggle the "Add a README file" option to On** and click **Create repository**.
4.  **Enable GitHub Pages**: Under your repository name, click **Settings**. In the left sidebar, under the "Code and automation" section, click **Pages**.
5.  **Choose your source**: Under "Build and deployment" > "Source", select **Deploy from a branch**. Then, under "Branch", use the dropdown menu to select your publishing source (usually the `main` or `master` branch).

> **Note**: After you push changes to GitHub, it can take up to 10 minutes for your site to publish.

### ✏️ Personalizing Your Site

Once your site is live, you can change its title and description by editing the `_config.yml` file in your repository:

1.  Click the **Code** tab of your repository.
2.  In the file list, click `_config.yml` to open it.
3.  Click the **edit icon** (pencil) to edit the file.
4.  Add a new line with `title:` followed by the title you want.
5.  Add a new line with `description:` followed by the description you want.
6.  When you are finished, click **Commit changes**.
