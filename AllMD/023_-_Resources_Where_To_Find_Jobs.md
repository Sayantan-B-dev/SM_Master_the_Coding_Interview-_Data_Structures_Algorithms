### 💎 My Favorites – Recommended Resources

#### 1. LinkedIn Jobs

The dominant platform for professional job searching, where recruiters actively seek candidates.

- **Market share**: LinkedIn captures nearly **80% of job saves**, with Indeed at ~10%.
- **How to use effectively**:
  - Optimize your profile with keywords from job descriptions you want.
  - Set up customised job alerts to apply quickly to new listings.
  - Use the free AI tool "Career Scout" in the Indeed app as a personal career coach.
  - Apply to jobs you feel slightly underqualified for – many descriptions are "wish lists".
- **Pro tip**: Recruiters pay for LinkedIn Recruiter to search for candidates, so a fully filled-out profile makes you discoverable.

#### 2. HackerNews – "Who is Hiring" Monthly Thread

A hidden treasure trove for developers and tech professionals, hosted on Hacker News every month.

- **What it is**: Each monthly thread gets **500-1000+ comments**, each one a real company posting real roles.
- **Why it's valuable**: You get direct insight into **salary trends, remote work patterns, and which technologies are in demand**.
- **How to use it**:
  - Search for the most recent "Ask HN: Who is hiring?" thread.
  - Browse comments directly or use **automated scrapers** that parse the unstructured data into searchable job listings.
  - Some developers have built GPT-powered tools that extract **company, role, location, salary, and contact info** from the raw comments.
- **Pro tip**: The job posts are from companies actively hiring developers – quality tends to be very high.

#### 3. Wellfound (formerly AngelList Talent)

The go-to platform for startup and tech jobs, particularly for early-stage companies and remote roles.

- **Key features**:
  - Connect directly with founders – **no third-party recruiters allowed**.
  - See **salary, stock options, and equity upfront** before applying.
  - **No cover letters needed** – your profile is your application.
  - One-click apply.
- **User feedback**: "I got my tech job on Wellfound 4 years ago and I'm still happy! Pays well, great culture, and unlimited PTO."
- **Best for**: Startups, tech roles, remote work, and transparent compensation.

### 🌍 Remote Jobs – Specialized Platforms

#### WeWorkRemotely

Describes itself as "the largest remote work community in the world".

- **Volume**: 300-500 active listings at any time, 50-75 new positions added daily, primarily in **programming (40%)**, design (20%), and customer support (15%).
- **Quality control**: Companies pay $299 for a 30-day listing, creating a natural filter that keeps out spam and low-effort postings.
- **What you won't find**: No profile building, no algorithms, no endless notifications – just clean, legitimate remote jobs.
- **Pro tip**: Subscribe to **category-specific RSS feeds** for real-time updates. Applying within 2 hours of posting significantly improves response rates.

#### Remote OK

A free, minimalist job board built specifically for remote-first work, created by digital nomad Pieter Levels.

- **Volume**: Over **50,000+ remote jobs posted annually** from 5,000+ active companies, with 100-150 new postings daily.
- **Key features**:
  - **Salary filter** – set minimum compensation thresholds to avoid wasting time on underpaid roles.
  - **Real-time feed** – new jobs appear instantly.
  - **RSS support** – monitor specific searches in your feed reader for real-time notifications.
  - **Cryptocurrency payment filtering** – useful for digital nomads.
- **Best for**: Tech, design, and marketing professionals seeking remote work.
- **User verdict**: "RemoteOK is one of the best places to find legit gigs, with fewer scams than many other online job boards."

### 🔧 Others – Additional Platforms to Consider

#### Whitetruffle

A **job matching platform** specifically for the tech industry, with a unique anonymous matching approach.

- **How it works**: Create an anonymous candidate profile. Employers contact you through the platform, and **your contact information remains private until you accept an interview request**.
- **Key feature**: Uses **AI to intelligently match** candidates with relevant job opportunities, streamlining the process.
- **User testimonial**: "Whitetruffle was the first and ONLY job matching tool I needed. After signing up for account I was contacted by a local company within the first 24 hours."
- **Best for**: Tech professionals who want to be discovered by employers without publicly sharing their information.

#### ZipRecruiter

A large, well-established job board with broad reach across many industries.

- **For job seekers**: **Completely free** – you can search jobs, apply, and manage your resume without paying anything.
- **Pros**: Huge number of job postings, large database of resumes, streamlined interface with easy navigation.
- **Cons**: Can generate excessive irrelevant email notifications; candidate quality can vary.
- **Best for**: Casting a wide net across multiple industries, especially for entry-level and mid-level roles.

#### Meetup.com

Not a traditional job board, but one of the **most effective networking tools** for finding jobs through referrals and relationships.

- **How to use it for job hunting**:
  - Search for **tech meetups in your city** by typing "tech" or "jobs" into Meetup's search bar with your location.
  - Attend events (virtual or in-person) to **connect directly with people who work at companies you're targeting**.
  - Many tech meetups include **job boards, career discussions, and hiring announcements**.
- **Why it works**: Referrals are the most effective way to get hired. Meetup events let you build genuine relationships before asking for job help.
- **Pro tip**: Join groups focused on **specific technologies** (React, Python, Data Science) – members often know about unadvertised openings.

### 🎯 Strategic Advice for Job Seekers

1. **Don't rely on a single platform**. Use a combination: LinkedIn for networking, Wellfound for startups, Remote OK for remote roles, and HackerNews for hidden tech opportunities.

2. **Apply early**. Many remote job postings on sites like WeWorkRemotely receive responses within hours of posting – timing matters significantly.

3. **Network offline too**. Meetup.com events can lead to referrals, which bypass the competitive online application process entirely.

4. **Follow application instructions precisely**. For job postings that require email submissions, use the email templates from the "Resources: Email" section and pay attention to subject line formatting.

5. **Check company reviews**. Before applying, research companies on Glassdoor to understand their culture, work-life balance, and compensation structures.

6. **Use RSS feeds for real-time alerts**. Platforms like WeWorkRemotely and Remote OK offer RSS feeds that give you an edge over candidates who check manually.
